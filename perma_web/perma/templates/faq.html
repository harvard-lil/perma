General/User FAQs

What is Perma.cc?

Perma.cc is a service that allows users to create citation links that will never break.

When a user creates a Perma.cc link, Perma.cc archives a copy of the referenced content, and generates a link to an unalterable hosted instance of the site. Regardless of what may happen to the original source, if the link is later published in a journal using the Perma.cc service, the archived version will always be available through the Perma.cc link.

How can I make a Perma.cc link?
Perma.cc links can be created on the front page. All it takes is the URL of the page you would like to archive. Once you enter your URL, we'll archive the content for you and give you a Perma.cc link that points to the archived content. If you've used a URL shortener (think bitly and the like) you'll recognize the "exchange my original URL for a new URL" pattern, just add "and archive the content at the URL" and you've fully grasped what Perma.cc does.
How can I keep track of my Perma.cc links?
The last few Perma.cc links that you created are shown at the bottom of the front page, as long as you are using a modern browser. If you have created an account and are logged in, you can see all your links in the management console.
Will you preserve my Perma.cc link forever?
When a link is first created, it is "unvested." Unvested Perma.cc links may be deleted from time to time, and are subject to page views to prevent abuse. A member of any vesting partner may verify that the link is actually being used in a scholarly publication. Once a link is vested, it will never be deleted unless we receive a legitimate legal order to take it down.
Can I ever delete a vested Perma.cc link?
No. Vested Perma.cc links can never be deleted once they are created.
How are links vested?
Users with vesting accounts can click on the "vest" button in the top-right of any Perma.cc page to vest it.
How do I acquire vesting privileges?
Vesting accounts are managed by scholarly journals and similar entities that are recognized by libraries. If you are a member of a journal and would like to use Perma.cc, please contact a library to sponsor you. You can see a list of participating libraries on the about Perma.cc page.
How can I contact Perma.cc?
Our feedback tool is the best way to contact us. If you'd prefer email, reach us at info@perma.cc

Librarian FAQs

How can my library become a partnering library?
If your library would like to become a Perma.cc library partner, email info@perma.cc, and you will be provided with a library “Registrar” account. This account will enable your library to create “Vesting Manager” accounts for the journals your library chooses to sponsor (traditionally the journals at your institution).
What does it mean to “sponsor” a journal?
Sponsoring a journal simply means that your library, as an institution, is certifying that a particular publication is a scholarly journal of the sort that a library would subscribe to and archive.
What are my responsibilities as a Perma.cc registrar?
As your library’s Perma.cc registrar, your primary responsibility is provisioning administrative accounts to journals (“Vesting Manager” accounts). Journals will be able to use those accounts to give access to their staff, so you won’t have to handle account management for all the students that work on a given journal. Depending on whether your library is also hosting Perma.cc content as an archive mirror, questions regarding your institution's notice and takedown policy may also be directed to you.
How do I make administrative accounts for journals at my school?
The process for making a Vesting Manager account is quick and simple. Once you have the email address that the journal would like associated with the account, go to the “Manage Users” section of your dashboard, click “Vesting Managers,” and provide the information requested under “Add a new vesting manager.”


Vesting Member FAQs

How do I vest a Perma.cc link?
To vest a Perma.cc link, simply follow the Perma.cc URL and click the “vest” button in the upper right-hand corner.
What if an author submits an article without Perma.cc links?
All vesting members (i.e. editors) will have the capability to both create and vest Perma.cc links. Thus, vesting members can create Perma.cc links for cited URLs as part of the subciting process.
How should I include the Perma.cc link in a citation?
Each journal has the ability to formulate its own standard. However, the recommended standard is to incorporate the Perma.cc link URL in brackets after the original URL.

Vesting Manager FAQs

How can I sign my journal up to use Perma.cc?
If your journal would like to become a Perma.cc journal partner, simply email info@perma.cc, and we will connect you with a sponsoring library (traditionally the library associated with your institution). The sponsoring library will create a journal manager account for your journal, which will enable you to create the accounts your staff will need to vest Perma.cc links.
What are my responsibilities as a vesting manager?
The main responsibilities of a vesting manager are to serve as a liaison between the journal and its sponsoring library and to provide staff with “Vesting Member” accounts, which will allow them to vest Perma.cc links.
How can I create accounts for my vesting members?
To create accounts for your staff members, go to the “Manage Users” tab of the dashboard, and click on “Journal Users.” Provide the requested information under “Add a new vesting member,” and click “Add vesting member.” Whether you make individual accounts for each staff member or communal accounts for those checking citations is up to your journal.

Technical FAQs

I want to be a Managed Partner. How do I configure my DNS?

You'll need to work with the person that manages your DNS to create an A record and point it to the Perma.cc IP address, 140.247.209.68 . Your A record might look like this,

perma.law.your-university.edu in A 140.247.209.68.

You can reference the Harvard Law School name as an example. Just do a dig on perma.law.harvard.edu

$ dig perma.law.harvard.edu

After you've added your A record, drop an email to info@perma.cc with your name and we'll whitelist you.

I don't want Perma.cc to archive a page from my site.

It's important to note that Perma.cc does not crawl your site like a search engine might. When Perma.cc is crawling your site, it is only interested in retrieving items that are needed to construct the single page it is archiving. This should be no more stressful for your webserver than a single user viewing a single page in a single browser.

If you don't want Perma.cc to display items from your site, add a rule for the PermaBot to your robots.txt

User-agent: PermaBot
Disallow: /private-stuff/

With that rule in place, Perma.cc will still archive the requested page from your site, but will not display it at Perma.cc.
