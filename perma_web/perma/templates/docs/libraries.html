Collaboration

Libraries naturally collaborate to maintain networks of accessible information. That collaborative nature animates Perma, which is supported by a consortium of library partners. Working together, these library partners can help maintain the integrity of scholarship and judicial opinions.


Preservation

Libraries have long led efforts to preserve important information against loss and destruction. As online sources become more prevalent, libraries are a natural home for digital preservation efforts like Perma.


Stewardship

Within Perma, libraries serve as custodians of the online sources that form the foundation of legal scholarship, judicial opinions and education. Acting as Registrars, libraries have an important responsibility to support and monitor the organizations affiliated with their institutions, and librarians can help curate the growing collection of materials archived using Perma.


Dissemination

Libraries also serve as a primary source for dissemination of materials archived using Perma. Just as libraries traditionally have served their patrons’ requests for access to physical materials, libraries participating in Perma play an important role in helping readers get access to the digital materials on which academic scholarship and judicial opinions increasingly rest.
