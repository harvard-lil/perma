Overview

Perma.cc is a service, currently in beta, that helps authors, editors and publishers protect their work - and their readers - from the ill effects of link rot.

Link rot (or “reference rot”) is the phenomenon by which material cited to on the Web vanishes or changes beyond recognition over time.

This problem is particularly serious in legal scholarship and judicial opinions. A recent study found that more than 70% of the links in a sample of law journals and 50% of the links in Supreme Court opinions no longer work.

Perma and its consortium of participating libraries aim to solve this problem.

By using Perma, you can archive web pages cited in your work. Each time you archive a web page, Perma generates a unique URL - a “Perma Link” - that will allow you to direct readers to the cited reference even if the original source goes away.
